## 1. Fundamental Physics Research

### 1.1 Classical Mechanics

**Core Concepts:**
- **Lagrangian Mechanics**: Action principle, generalized coordinates, Euler-Lagrange equations
- **Hamiltonian Mechanics**: Hamilton's equations, phase space, canonical transformations
- **Rigid Body Dynamics**: Angular momentum, moments of inertia, Euler angles
- **Central Force Problems**: Kepler's laws, gravitational dynamics
- **Continuous Systems**: Field theory foundations, continuum mechanics

**Key Resources:**
- MIT 8.09 Classical Mechanics III: Covers Lagrangian and Hamiltonian formalism, constraints, rigid body dynamics, Hamilton-Jacobi theory, action-angle variables, perturbation theory
- MIT 8.223 Classical Mechanics II: Hamiltonian dynamics, Legendre transforms

**Simulation Relevance:**
- Foundation for all macroscopic motion
- Conservation laws (energy, momentum, angular momentum)
- Basis for particle dynamics in classical limit

### 1.2 Electromagnetism

**Core Concepts:**
- **Maxwell's Equations**: Gauss's law, Faraday's law, Ampère's law with Maxwell's correction
- **Electromagnetic Fields**: Electric and magnetic field dynamics, wave propagation
- **Potential Theory**: Scalar and vector potentials, gauge invariance
- **Electrodynamics**: Relativistic formulation, electromagnetic radiation

**Simulation Relevance:**
- Electromagnetic interactions between charged particles
- Light propagation and cosmological redshift
- Plasma physics and magnetic fields in astrophysics

### 1.3 Thermodynamics & Statistical Mechanics

**Core Concepts:**
- **Laws of Thermodynamics**: Energy conservation, entropy, temperature
- **Statistical Ensembles**: Microcanonical, canonical, grand canonical
- **Kinetic Theory**: Boltzmann equation, transport phenomena
- **Phase Transitions**: Critical phenomena, order parameters

**Simulation Relevance:**
- Early universe thermodynamics (Big Bang nucleosynthesis)
- Stellar evolution and nuclear fusion
- Large-scale structure formation and cooling

### 1.4 Quantum Mechanics

**Core Concepts:**
- **Wave-Particle Duality**: De Broglie wavelength, complementarity principle
- **Schrödinger Equation**: Time evolution, stationary states
- **Quantum States**: Wavefunctions, probability amplitudes
- **Observables and Measurement**: Operators, eigenvalues, uncertainty principle
- **Entanglement**: Non-local correlations, Bell's theorem

**Simulation Relevance:**
- Quantum fields in the early universe
- Particle creation/annihilation processes
- Quantum coherence on cosmological scales (debatable)

### 1.5 Special & General Relativity

**Core Concepts:**
- **Special Relativity**: Lorentz transformations, spacetime interval, relativistic dynamics
- **General Relativity**: Einstein field equations, spacetime curvature, geodesics
- **Black Holes**: Event horizons, singularities, Hawking radiation
- **Cosmological Solutions**: FRW metric, Friedmann equations, expansion dynamics

**Simulation Relevance:**
- Fundamental to large-scale universe simulation
- Gravitational dynamics and structure formation
- Light propagation and cosmological observations
- Black hole physics and extreme environments

### 1.6 Cosmology

**Core Concepts:**
- **Big Bang Theory**: Initial singularity, cosmic inflation, expansion history
- **Cosmic Microwave Background**: Primordial fluctuations, acoustic peaks
- **Large-Scale Structure**: Galaxy clusters, cosmic web, dark matter halos
- **Dark Energy**: Cosmological constant, accelerated expansion
- **Cosmological Parameters**: Hubble constant, density parameters, curvature

**Simulation Relevance:**
- Central framework for universe simulation
- Sets initial conditions for all physics
- Determines large-scale evolution and final state

### 1.7 Astrophysics

**Core Concepts:**
- **Stellar Evolution**: Main sequence, supernovae, neutron stars, black holes
- **Galaxy Formation**: Dark matter halos, baryonic processes, mergers
- **High-Energy Astrophysics**: Pulsars, quasars, gamma-ray bursts
- **Exoplanets**: Formation, detection, atmospheric physics

**Simulation Relevance:**
- Realistic stellar populations and luminosity functions
- Galaxy-scale physics and feedback mechanisms
- Transient phenomena and extreme events

### 1.8 Particle Physics

**Core Concepts:**
- **Standard Model**: Quarks, leptons, gauge bosons, Higgs mechanism
- **Quantum Field Theory**: Lagrangian formalism, Feynman diagrams, renormalization
- **Symmetry Breaking**: Electroweak symmetry, CP violation
- **Beyond Standard Model**: Supersymmetry, dark matter candidates

**Simulation Relevance:**
- Early universe particle physics (baryogenesis, nucleosynthesis)
- Dark matter physics and interactions
- High-energy processes in extreme environments

### 1.9 String Theory / Loop Quantum Gravity

**Core Concepts:**
- **String Theory**: Extra dimensions, vibrational modes, holographic principle
- **Loop Quantum Gravity**: Spin networks, discrete spacetime, quantum geometry
- **Quantum Gravity Problem**: Reconciliation of QM and GR

**Simulation Relevance:**
- Theoretical foundations for Planck-scale physics
- Potential modifications to early universe cosmology
- Speculative but could guide simulation design at smallest scales

## 4. Simulation Architecture Considerations

### 4.1 Scale Hierarchy
1. **Quantum Scale** (10^-35 m - 10^-15 m): Planck scale, particle physics
2. **Atomic Scale** (10^-10 m - 10^-8 m): Atomic and molecular physics
3. **Macroscopic Scale** (10^-6 m - 10^6 m): Everyday physics, fluid dynamics
4. **Astrophysical Scale** (10^9 m - 10^21 m): Stars, solar systems
5. **Cosmological Scale** (10^22 m - 10^26 m): Galaxies, universe

### 4.2 Physics Integration Challenges
- **Multi-Scale Coupling**: Different physics at different scales
- **Computational Complexity**: Simulating quantum + classical scales simultaneously
- **Approximation Strategies**: When can classical mechanics replace quantum?
- **Numerical Stability**: Long-term evolution without numerical artifacts

### 4.3 Computational Requirements
- **Spatial Resolution**: Adaptive mesh refinement, particle-in-cell
- **Time Integration**: Multiple time scales, symplectic integrators for energy conservation
- **Parallelization**: Distributed computing, GPU acceleration
- **Data Management**: Huge datasets, visualization pipelines
