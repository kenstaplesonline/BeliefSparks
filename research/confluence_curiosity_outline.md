## Website Vision & Brand

### Concept
Confluence Curiosity is a science storytelling website exploring the unique phenomena that shape Calgary and its surrounding region. Inspired by National Geographic and Smithsonian Institution, with authentic Calgary character and regional focus.

### Mission
To make Calgary science accessible, engaging, and relevant to locals and visitors through compelling storytelling, stunning visuals, and interactive exploration.

### Target Audience
- **Primary:** Calgarians and southern Alberta residents
- **Secondary:** Visitors, students, educators, weather enthusiasts
- **Tertiary:** Researchers, journalists, policymakers

### Design Inspiration
- **Visual Style:** Natgeo.com and si.edu
- **Regional Flavor:** Calgary-specific photography, local interviews, prairie perspectives
- **Tone:** Curious, authoritative, accessible, occasionally playful

## Section 1: Phenomena (Chinook Winds)

### Page: "The Snow Eater: Understanding Chinook Winds"

#### Introduction (Hero)
- **Headline:** "The Snow Eater: How Calgary's Chinook Winds Transform Winter"
- **Subhead:** "In hours, temperatures can soar from -20°C to +20°C. Here's the science behind the phenomenon."
- **Visual:** Dramatic Chinook arch over Calgary skyline
- **Interactive Element:** Temperature swing slider showing before/during/after

#### Module 1: What Is a Chinook?
- **Definition:** Föhn wind, warm/dry westerly winds from the Rockies
- **Pronunciation:** "Shin-uk" in Alberta (interactive audio pronunciation)
- **Etymology:** From the Chinook people near Columbia River
- **Blackfoot Name:** "Snow Eater" (with cultural context)
- **Global Family:** Comparison with Foehn (Europe), Zonda (Argentina), Berg (South Africa)

#### Module 2: The Science (Interactive)
- **Step-by-Step Animation:**
  1. Pacific moisture loading
  2. Orographic lift (air rises up mountains)
  3. Precipitation release (snow/rain on western slopes)
  4. Dry adiabatic warming (air descends eastern slopes)
  5. Warm air reaches prairies
- **Temperature Rates:**
  - Moist adiabatic: 5°C/1000m
  - Dry adiabatic: 10°C/1000m
  - Net warming: illustrated with thermometer animation

#### Module 3: Where Do They Happen?
- **Interactive Map:**
  - Hotspots: Pincher Creek, Lethbridge, Crowsnest Pass (highlighted)
  - Calgary: Core Chinook belt position
  - Geographic reach: High Level (north) to Las Vegas (south)
  - Frequency overlay: 30-35 days/year in southern Alberta
- **Historical Data:** Clickable weather station data points

#### Module 4: Visual Signature - The Chinook Arch
- **Gallery:** Stunning Chinook arch photography
- **Color Science:** Explanation of light refraction through clouds
- **Time-of-Day Guide:** Morning colors → Midday grey → Sunset hues
- **User Submissions:** "Upload Your Arch Shot" feature

#### Module 5: Extreme Records
- **Infographic: Temperature Extremes**
  - Pincher Creek 1966: +25.5°C in one hour
  - Claresholm 1992: 24°C in February
  - Loma, MT 1972: 57°C change in 24 hours
- **Comparison:** Calgary's average vs. Chinook-enhanced highs
- **Timeline:** Interactive slider through historic events

#### Module 6: Wind Speed & Duration
- **Data Visualization:**
  - Typical range: 16-60 km/h
  - Gust peaks: Up to 100 km/h
  - Duration distribution histogram
- **Safety Tips:** Driving warnings, wind damage prep

## Section 3: Interactives

### Interactive 1: "Chinook Simulator"
- **User Experience:**

  - See predicted arch formation

## Technical Implementation Notes

### Platform Considerations
- **CMS Options:** WordPress (with custom theme), Ghost, or custom Next.js build
- **Mobile-First:** Responsive design essential for on-the-go weather checking
- **Performance:** Image optimization critical for photography-heavy site
- **Accessibility:** WCAG 2.1 AA compliance, alt text for all images

### Visual Design System
- **Color Palette:**
  - Primary: Chinook Arch pinks/oranges (warm, dynamic)
  - Secondary: Prairie grass gold and sky blue
  - Accent: Calgary stampede red for CTAs
- **Typography:**
  - Headings: Bold, display fonts (for visual impact)
  - Body: Clean, readable sans-serif
- **Photography Style:**
  - Wide, dramatic landscapes
  - Human-centric shots (faces, hands, weather-appropriate clothing)
  - Before/during/after sequences

### Data Visualization Strategy
- **Library:** D3.js or Chart.js for interactive charts
- **Maps:** Leaflet.js with custom styling
- **Animations:** CSS transitions and minimal JS for performance

### Content Management Workflow
- **Editorial Calendar:** Monthly planning with seasonal themes
- **Review Process:** Scientific accuracy vetting before publication
- **Archive Strategy:** Permanent URLs for research citation
- **Version Control:** Content updates with change logs

## Conclusion

This outline provides a comprehensive roadmap for Confluence Curiosity's launch, anchored in the rich scientific and cultural territory of Calgary's Chinook winds. The website will balance rigorous science with accessible storytelling, engage the community through interactive features and personal narratives, and create a digital resource that celebrates the unique phenomena that define life on the prairies.

The result will be a destination for Calgarians and visitors to understand, appreciate, and engage with the science that shapes our region—one story, one phenomenon, one wonder at a time.
